How Much Do Impact Windows Really Save on Florida Insurance in 2026?

FIS
Florida Impact Shield Team
June 2026 · 8 min read

If you're a Florida homeowner, you already know insurance premiums have become one of the biggest financial pressures in the state. The average Florida homeowners insurance premium hit $9,462 in 2025 — more than 3× the national average. For coastal counties, it's worse.

Impact windows are the single most effective tool to bring that number down — permanently. Here's exactly how much you'll save, backed by data.

📋 The legal foundation: Florida Statute 627.0629 mandates that all admitted insurance companies in Florida must offer premium discounts for wind mitigation features. Impact windows qualify for the highest tier. Your insurer has no discretion — the discount is legally required once you submit a wind mitigation inspection report.

How the Insurance Discount Actually Works

After your impact windows are installed, a licensed wind mitigation inspector completes Form OIR-B1-1802, documenting your opening protections. You submit this to your insurer, and they're legally required to apply the discount at your next renewal (or immediately mid-term in many cases).

The discount applies to the windstorm portion of your premium — typically 40–60% of your total bill. This is where the biggest dollars live. A 35% reduction on the windstorm portion of a $10,000 policy can mean $3,500 back in your pocket every year.

How to Maximize Your Insurance Discount

  • Install all openings. Partial protection (some windows but not all) still qualifies for a discount, but not the maximum tier. Full replacement of all windows and doors gets you the highest possible reduction.
  • Use HVHZ-certified products in applicable counties. In Miami-Dade and Broward, products must carry a Miami-Dade Notice of Acceptance (NOA). We only install NOA-approved products.
  • File the wind mitigation report quickly. You can request a mid-term policy adjustment — don't wait for renewal. Your insurer must process the discount within 30 days.
  • Combine with My Safe Florida Home grant. The grant reduces your out-of-pocket cost, improving your payback timeline dramatically. See our grant guide →

💰 Bottom line: On a typical Florida home with a $8,500/year premium, impact windows save $2,100–$3,400 per year. Over 10 years, that's $21,000–$34,000 in insurance savings alone — before counting storm damage prevention, home value increase, or elimination of shutter costs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does every Florida insurer have to give me the discount?

Yes — all admitted (state-approved) insurers in Florida must apply wind mitigation discounts under Florida Statute 627.0629. Surplus lines carriers may differ; check your policy declarations page.

How soon does my premium drop after installation?

You can request a mid-term endorsement immediately after your wind mitigation inspection. Most insurers apply the discount within 15–30 days and issue a refund or credit for the overpaid portion.

Do impact windows affect my deductible?

Impact windows reduce your windstorm premium, but your hurricane deductible (typically 2–5% of dwelling value) is a separate policy term. Some insurers will lower the deductible percentage as well — ask your agent specifically about this.
