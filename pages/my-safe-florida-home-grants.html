## Understanding Impact Windows in Florida's Hurricane Zones Florida homeowners face unique challenges when it comes to protecting their properties from severe weather. As a state surrounded by water and located in one of the most active hurricane zones in the world, investing in proper storm protection isn't just recommended—it's essential. **Impact windows Florida** installations have become the gold standard for hurricane protection, offering superior defense against high winds, flying debris, and the catastrophic damage that accompanies tropical storms and hurricanes. Unlike traditional windows with external shutters, impact resistant windows provide year-round protection without the need for last-minute storm preparations. These specialized windows feature two layers of glass bonded to a durable interlayer, typically made of polyvinyl butyral (PVB) or ethylene-vinyl acetate (EVA). When debris strikes the window during a storm, the glass may crack, but the interlayer holds the shards in place, maintaining the building envelope's integrity. ## Florida Building Code Requirements for Hurricane Windows The Florida Building Code (FBC) establishes strict requirements for window installations in hurricane-prone regions. According to Florida Statute 553.73, all new construction and replacement windows in the High-Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ) must meet specific impact resistance standards. These standards are outlined in FBC Section 1609 and require windows to withstand large and small missile impact tests. Impact windows installed throughout Florida must comply with: - ASTM E1996 and ASTM E1886 testing protocols - Miami-Dade County Notice of Acceptance (NOA) certification for HVHZ areas - Florida Product Approval system requirements - Design pressure ratings appropriate for your specific wind zone As a licensed CBC contractor, Florida Impact Shield ensures all installations meet or exceed these stringent requirements, providing homeowners with peace of mind and insurance certification compliance. ## Massive Insurance Savings with Impact Windows Florida One of the most compelling reasons to install **impact windows Florida** is the substantial reduction in homeowners insurance premiums.
