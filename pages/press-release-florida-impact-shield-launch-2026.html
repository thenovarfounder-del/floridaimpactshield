## Florida Impact Shield Announces Statewide Solution to Hurricane Insurance Crisis: Homeowners Cutting Premiums Up to 45% With State-Backed Impact Window Program

### Licensed CBC Contractor Combines MSFH Grants, Zero-Interest Financing, and AI Technology to Make Hurricane Protection Affordable Across All 67 Florida Counties

**Miami, FL — June 15, 2026** — As Florida homeowners face crushing insurance premium increases averaging 102% since 2019, Florida Impact Shield today announced an aggressive statewide expansion that puts mandated insurance savings of up to 45% within reach for every Florida homeowner through professionally installed impact-resistant windows and doors.

The comprehensive solution addresses Florida's insurance crisis head-on by combining available My Safe Florida Home (MSFH) grants of up to $10,000, zero-percent financing options, and expert installation services across all 67 Florida counties — from Escambia to Miami-Dade, from the Panhandle to the Keys.

Florida law mandates that insurance carriers reduce premiums for homes fortified with impact-resistant openings, with savings ranging from 30% to 45% depending on the property's location and construction. For many homeowners paying $6,000 to $12,000 annually in hurricane insurance, the savings can exceed $2,000 to $5,000 per year — often covering the cost of impact window installation within three to five years.

"Florida's insurance crisis has forced families to choose between adequate coverage and their monthly budget," a Florida Impact Shield spokesperson said. "We're eliminating that impossible choice. Every homeowner deserves access to the protection and savings that Florida law guarantees them, and we've built the infrastructure to deliver it statewide."

The timing proves critical. Six insurance carriers have exited Florida since 2022, and Citizens Property Insurance Corporation — the state-backed insurer of last resort — now covers more than 1.3 million policies. Insurance industry analysts project another turbulent hurricane season could trigger additional carrier departures and premium spikes exceeding 40% for 2027.

Florida Impact Shield's service model removes traditional barriers that have prevented homeowners from accessing available relief programs:

**MSFH Grant Navigation**: The company guides homeowners through the My Safe Florida Home program application process, helping secure grants up to $10,000 for impact window and door installation. These state-funded grants require no repayment.

**Zero-Percent Financing**: For costs exceeding grant amounts, Florida Impact Shield offers zero-interest financing plans that make monthly payments lower than the insurance savings homeowners receive — creating immediate positive cash flow.

**Statewide Licensed Coverage**: As a state-certified Building Contractor (CBC) licensed since 2018, Florida Impact Shield maintains the credentials, insurance, and bonding required for installations across all Florida counties, from rural Panhandle communities to dense urban centers.

**24/7 AI-Powered Service**: The company's Eva AI chatbot provides round-the-clock answers to homeowner questions about products, pricing, grant eligibility, insurance requirements, and installation timelines, ensuring no homeowner faces confusion or delays.

The company has earned a 4.9 out of 5.0 customer rating based on installation quality, project timeline adherence, and post-installation support. Each installation includes products that meet or exceed Florida Building Code requirements for high-velocity hurricane zones.

"We're not just installing windows — we're installing financial relief and peace of mind," a Florida Impact Shield spokesperson said. "When a homeowner sees their insurance premium drop $3,500 while knowing their family is protected by the strongest hurricane defense available, that's when this work becomes meaningful. We're doing that thousands of times across Florida."

The impact window and door installations provide multiple benefits beyond insurance savings. Homeowners report reduced energy costs averaging 20% to 30% due to improved insulation, elimination of exterior storm shutters and pre-storm preparation stress, enhanced security against break-ins, and significant noise reduction from exterior sounds.

Florida Impact Shield serves residential properties across all structural types, including single-family homes, townhomes, condominiums (with association approval), and manufactured homes built after 1994. The company provides free property assessments, transparent pricing, and detailed documentation required for insurance premium recalculation.

The statewide expansion comes as Florida legislators consider additional homeowner relief measures for the 2027 session, including expanded MSFH funding and potential tax credits for hurricane mitigation improvements.
