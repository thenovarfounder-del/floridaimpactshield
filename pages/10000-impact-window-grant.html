The $10,000 Florida Impact Window Grant — How to Get It

Florida's $10,000 Impact Window Grant — Get Free Money to Protect Your Home in 2026

If you've been putting off impact windows because of the cost, the State of Florida is handing homeowners up to $10,000 in free grant money right now — and you don't have to pay a single dollar of it back. The My Safe Florida Home program is one of the most powerful home improvement grant programs in the country, and with $352 million in active 2026 funding already available, the window to claim your grant is open today. Homeowners across all 67 Florida counties are eligible, and every day you wait is a day someone else claims the funding that could have been yours.

At Florida Impact Shield, we work exclusively with homeowners applying for the My Safe Florida Home grant. This page will walk you through exactly how the $10,000 impact window grant works, who qualifies, what it covers, and how to get started today.

What Is the $10,000 My Safe Florida Home Grant?

The My Safe Florida Home program is a state-funded initiative administered by the Florida Department of Financial Services. Its goal is simple: help Florida homeowners harden their homes against hurricane damage by subsidizing the cost of impact windows, impact doors, hurricane shutters, roof reinforcement, and qualifying garage doors.

Here's how the grant math works for most homeowners:

  • The state pays $2 for every $1 you spend on qualifying improvements
  • The maximum grant award is $10,000
  • This means you need to spend at least $5,000 out of pocket to unlock the full $10,000
  • You are never repaying this money — it is a true grant, not a loan

For low-income qualifying homeowners, the deal gets even better. If your household income falls below the program's low-income threshold, the state covers the full $10,000 with NO personal match required. That means the entire job could be covered at zero cost to you.

💰 This Is Not a Loan — There Is No Repayment

Many homeowners confuse grants with financing. The My Safe Florida Home $10,000 grant is 100% free money from the State of Florida. You will never receive a bill. There is no interest rate. There is no repayment schedule. The state simply pays your contractor directly for the qualifying work — and you keep the savings, the protection, and the lower insurance premiums forever.

What Does the $10,000 Grant Cover?

The My Safe Florida Home grant is specifically designed to fund hurricane hardening improvements. Qualifying expenses under the program include:

  • Impact-resistant windows (the most popular use of the grant)
  • Impact-resistant exterior doors, including sliding glass doors
  • Hurricane shutters for all openings
  • Reinforced garage doors rated for hurricane-force winds
  • Roof deck attachment and secondary water resistance improvements
  • Opening protection systems including accordion shutters and storm panels

Most Florida homeowners use the grant specifically for impact windows because they deliver the broadest protection, eliminate the need for seasonal shutter installation, and generate the largest insurance premium discounts available under Florida law.

Who Qualifies for the Florida $10,000 Impact Window Grant?

The My Safe Florida Home grant has clear eligibility requirements. You must meet all of the following criteria:

  • Florida homestead exemption — this must be your primary residence
  • Home built before 2008 — homes constructed after the 2007 Florida Building Code update are not eligible
  • Insured property value under $700,000 — based on your homeowner's insurance coverage amount
  • Current homeowner's insurance — you must maintain active coverage on the property
  • Income eligibility — to qualify for the full no-match $10,000 award, your household income must fall within low-income guidelines; standard applicants qualify for the 2:1 matching grant regardless of income
  • Florida county of residence — all 67 Florida counties are eligible, from Escambia to Miami-Dade

If you're unsure whether you qualify, the best step is to submit your application and schedule your free state inspection — the program handles verification for you.

📅 2026 Funding Is Available Right Now — But It Won't Last

The Florida Legislature has allocated $352 million in My Safe Florida Home funding for 2026, with an additional $405 million more currently being approved for future allocation. Despite these large numbers, the grant is awarded on a first-come, first-served basis. During the 2023 funding cycle, the program ran out of money within months of opening. Homeowners who waited lost their chance entirely. The time to apply is now — not after the next storm puts everyone in line ahead of you.

The 5 Steps to Claim Your $10,000 Florida Impact Window Grant

The process is straightforward and Florida Impact Shield guides you through every step. Here's exactly how it works:

  1. Apply Online Through the Official My Safe Florida Home Portal — Submit your application at the state's official program website. You'll create an account, enter your property details, and confirm your eligibility information. This takes about 15–20 minutes.
  2. Schedule Your Free State-Certified Home Inspection — Once your application is approved, a certified inspector comes to your home at no cost. They assess your current windows, doors, and roof to determine what hardening improvements qualify and what your grant award amount will be.
  3. Get Your Contractor Quote — After your inspection report is issued, you work with a state-approved contractor like Florida Impact Shield to receive a detailed quote for your qualifying improvements. Your quote must match the scope identified in the inspection report.
  4. Grant Approval and Award Letter — The state reviews your contractor quote and issues your official grant award letter confirming the amount of your grant. This is when your $10,000 award is locked in.
  5. Installation and Direct Payment — Your contractor completes the installation of your impact windows, doors, or other qualifying improvements. The state pays the contractor directly for the grant portion — you only pay your share at project completion.

Grant Savings PLUS Insurance Savings — The Double Win

The $10,000 grant is only half of the financial story. Once your impact windows are installed, you become eligible for significant reductions on your Florida homeowner's insurance premium. Florida law requires insurers to offer wind mitigation discounts to homeowners who have completed qualifying upgrades — and insurance savings from hurricane impact windows can reduce your annual premium by hundreds or even thousands of dollars every single year.

Think about it this way: the state pays up to $10,000 of your installation cost upfront, and then your insurance company pays you back through reduced premiums every year going forward. Over a 10-year period, many Florida homeowners recover their entire out-of-pocket cost through insurance savings alone — meaning your impact windows effectively pay for themselves while protecting everything you own.

All 67 Florida Counties Are Eligible

No matter where in Florida you live, the My Safe Florida Home grant is available to you. This includes high-risk coastal counties like Miami-Dade, Broward, Palm Beach, Pinellas, Hillsborough, Lee, Collier, Volusia, Brevard, and St. Lucie, as well as inland counties throughout Central and North Florida. If your home has a Florida homestead exemption and was built before 2008, your county of residence is not a limiting factor.

Frequently Asked Questions About the $10,000 Florida Impact Window Grant

Do I have to pay back the My Safe Florida Home grant?

No. The My Safe Florida Home $10,000 grant is not a loan and requires zero repayment. It is a direct grant from the State of Florida. Once the installation is complete and the contractor is paid, there is nothing further owed by the homeowner under any circumstances.

Can renters apply for the $10,000 impact window grant?

No. The grant is available exclusively to owner-occupants with a valid Florida homestead exemption on the property. The home must be your primary residence and you must own it. Rental properties and investment properties are not eligible.

What if my home was built after 2008?

Homes built after 2007 are not eligible for the My Safe Florida Home grant because they were already required to be built to the updated Florida Building Code, which includes improved wind resistance standards. The grant targets older homes that were built before those requirements existed.

How long does the grant application process take?

From application submission to installation, the process typically takes between 60 and 120 days depending on current application volume and inspector scheduling in your area. Because funding is first-come, first-served, submitting your application as early as possible protects your place in line even if the process takes a few months to complete.

Can I use the grant for impact windows AND impact doors on the same project?

Yes. The $10,000 grant can be applied to a combination of qualifying improvements on the same project, including impact windows, impact doors, hurricane shutters, and garage door upgrades. The total grant award is capped at $10,000, but that $10,000 can cover multiple types of qualifying hurricane protection improvements in a single project.
