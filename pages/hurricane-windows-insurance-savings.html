Hurricane Windows Insurance Savings in Florida: The Complete 2024 Guide

If you're a Florida homeowner, you already know that insurance premiums have skyrocketed in recent years. What you might not know is that installing hurricane-rated impact windows can reduce your annual homeowners insurance by 27% to 45%, depending on your county. This comprehensive guide explains exactly how Florida's mandatory insurance discount laws work, what documentation you need, and how to maximize your return on investment through combined savings, grants, and financing options.

Florida Statute 627.0629: Your Legal Right to Insurance Discounts

Florida Statute 627.0629 is the law that requires all insurance companies operating in Florida to provide premium discounts for hurricane protection features, including impact-resistant windows and doors. Enacted to encourage property owners to strengthen their homes against windstorm damage, this statute mandates that insurers offer specific credits for wind mitigation features that have been verified through an official inspection.

The law specifically covers opening protection (windows, doors, garage doors, and skylights) and requires insurers to actuarially justify their rates while providing credits for protective features. This isn't an optional discount that insurers can choose to offer—it's a legal requirement. Insurance companies must file their wind mitigation discount schedules with the Florida Office of Insurance Regulation, ensuring standardized savings across the state.

Understanding this statute empowers you as a homeowner. When you install hurricane windows, you're not asking for a favor from your insurance company—you're claiming a legally mandated discount that reflects the reduced risk your fortified home presents.

Wind Mitigation Form OIR-B1-1802: Your Key to Unlocking Savings

The Wind Mitigation Inspection Uniform Mitigation Verification Form OIR-B1-1802 is the official document that unlocks your insurance discounts. This standardized form, created by the Florida Office of Insurance Regulation, documents all wind-resistant features of your home that qualify for premium reductions.

What the Form Includes

The OIR-B1-1802 form captures critical information about your home's wind resistance, including:

  • Opening Protection Rating: Documents whether windows, doors, and other openings meet Miami-Dade or Florida Building Code requirements for impact resistance
  • Roof Covering: The installation date and attachment method of your roof
  • Roof Deck Attachment: How your roof deck is secured to the structure
  • Roof-to-Wall Attachment: The type of connection between your roof and walls (clips, single wraps, double wraps, or toe nails)
  • Secondary Water Resistance: Whether your roof has a sealed deck
  • Roof Geometry: The shape of your roof, which affects wind resistance

How to File Form OIR-B1-1802

After your hurricane windows are installed, you'll need to schedule a wind mitigation inspection with a licensed inspector. These professionals are typically licensed home inspectors, general contractors, or engineers authorized to complete the OIR-B1-1802 form. The inspection usually takes 30-45 minutes and costs between $75 and $150.

Once completed, you'll receive the official form with the inspector's seal and signature. Submit this form directly to your insurance company or through your insurance agent. Learn more about the inspection process on our wind mitigation inspection page.

Important: Keep multiple copies of your completed OIR-B1-1802 form. You'll need it when switching insurance companies, refinancing your home, or if your insurer requests updated documentation. The form is typically valid for five years.

Citizens Insurance vs. Private Carriers: Understanding Discount Differences

Citizens Property Insurance Corporation, Florida's state-backed insurer of last resort, often provides more aggressive wind mitigation discounts than private carriers, particularly in high-risk coastal counties. However, the state has been actively working to depopulate Citizens by encouraging private market competition.

Citizens typically offers the maximum allowable discounts under Florida law, sometimes reaching 45-50% in South Florida when all wind mitigation features are present. Private carriers may offer 35-42% discounts for the same features, though this varies by company. However, even with slightly lower discount percentages, private carriers often start with lower base premiums, potentially resulting in similar or better absolute dollar savings.

When comparing quotes, always calculate the final premium amount after discounts rather than focusing solely on discount percentages. A 40% discount on a $7,000 premium saves more than a 45% discount on a $6,000 premium.

Pro Tip: After installing hurricane windows and obtaining your wind mitigation form, shop your insurance with at least three carriers. The competitive market combined with your improved wind mitigation credits often reveals significant savings opportunities beyond your current insurer's discount.

The Wind Mitigation Inspection Process Explained

Understanding how the wind mitigation inspection works helps you prepare for maximum savings. The inspection evaluates your entire home's wind resistance, not just your windows, though opening protection typically provides the largest single discount.

Who Conducts Wind Mitigation Inspections

Licensed professionals authorized to complete Form OIR-B1-1802 include:

  • Florida-licensed home inspectors with wind mitigation certification
  • Licensed general contractors
  • Licensed building contractors
  • Professional engineers licensed in Florida
  • Architects licensed in Florida

What the Inspector Examines

During the inspection, the professional will photograph and document your impact windows, checking for proper labeling and permits. For opening protection, they'll verify that windows meet either Miami-Dade County Product Approval or Florida Building Code approval standards. They'll also inspect your attic to evaluate roof attachment, examine exterior features, and verify that all openings (including garage doors and entry doors) are protected.

The entire process typically takes 30-45 minutes for an average home. Visit our detailed wind mitigation inspection guide for more information about preparing for your inspection.

Timeline from Installation to Premium Reduction

Understanding the timeline helps you plan and set realistic expectations for when insurance savings begin:

  • Week 1-4: Hurricane window installation completed and final permits closed
  • Week 5: Schedule wind mitigation inspection with licensed inspector
  • Week 6: Receive completed OIR-B1-1802 form from inspector
  • Week 7: Submit form to insurance company or agent
  • Week 8-10: Insurance company processes form and adjusts premium
  • Week 11: Receive revised policy declaration with reduced premium

Most homeowners see their premium reduction within 60-75 days of window installation completion. If your policy renewal is approaching, time your installation to complete the process before renewal for immediate full-year savings. Mid-policy installations typically result in a prorated refund for the remaining policy term.

▼ How long does it take to recoup my investment in hurricane windows through insurance savings alone?

Payback periods vary by county and investment size but typically range from 8-15 years through insurance savings alone. In high-risk coastal counties like Miami-Dade and Broward where discounts reach 40-45%, homeowners often recoup their investment in 10-12 years. When factoring in home value appreciation, grants, and avoided deductibles from storm damage, the effective payback period drops to 5-8 years. Remember that insurance premiums continue rising annually, accelerating your payback timeline.

▼ Will I lose my insurance discount if I switch insurance companies?

No, your wind mitigation features and discounts transfer between insurance companies. Your completed OIR-B1-1802 form remains valid (typically for five years) and can be submitted to any new insurer. In fact, shopping for insurance after installing hurricane windows often reveals additional savings as different carriers offer varying discount structures. Always provide your wind mitigation form when obtaining quotes from new insurers to ensure accurate pricing.

▼ Can I get a discount for installing hurricane windows on only part of my home?

Insurance discounts for opening protection require that all openings in your home's building envelope be protected to the same standard. You cannot receive the full opening protection credit for protecting only certain windows or one side of your home. However, upgrading your most vulnerable openings (like large picture windows or sliding glass doors) as a first phase while planning to complete the rest later is a common approach. Just understand that the insurance discount only applies once all openings meet code requirements.

▼ Do I need a new wind mitigation inspection if I had one before installing hurricane windows?

Yes, you must obtain an updated wind mitigation inspection after installing hurricane windows to document the improvement and claim the enhanced discount. Your previous inspection showed your home's condition before the upgrade. The new inspection captures the improved opening protection rating, which triggers the additional insurance discount. The cost of this inspection ($75-$150) is a small investment that unlocks thousands in annual savings.

▼ Are insurance discounts for hurricane windows guaranteed to stay the same every year?

While Florida Statute 627.0629 mandates that insurers provide wind mitigation discounts, the specific percentage amounts can change if insurers refile their rates with the Florida Office of Insurance Regulation. However, these discount schedules are reviewed actuarially and tend to remain stable. Even if percentages adjust slightly, your absolute dollar savings typically increase over time as base premiums rise. The wind resistance features you've installed continue providing value regardless of minor discount schedule adjustments.
