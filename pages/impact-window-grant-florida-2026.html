Understanding Impact Window Grant Eligibility in Florida

To qualify for impact window grant funding in Florida for 2026, homeowners must meet specific criteria. Most programs require that your home be your primary residence, built before the current Florida Building Code was implemented in your county, and located in a designated hurricane risk zone. Income restrictions may apply to certain grant programs, though many mitigation grants are available regardless of household income.

Property insurance status also plays a role—homeowners must maintain an active homeowners insurance policy to qualify for most grant programs. The good news is that installing impact windows through these grant programs can reduce your insurance premiums by 15-45%, creating long-term savings that compound year after year.

Insurance Premium Reductions: The Gift That Keeps Giving

Beyond the immediate grant funding, impact windows deliver substantial long-term financial benefits through reduced insurance premiums.
