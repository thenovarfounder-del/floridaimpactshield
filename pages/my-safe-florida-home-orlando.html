What Is the My Safe Florida Home Grant? A Central Florida Overview

The My Safe Florida Home (MSFH) grant program is a state-funded initiative administered by the Florida Department of Financial Services designed to help Florida homeowners harden their homes against hurricane damage. For Orlando-area homeowners, this program represents one of the most significant financial opportunities available in 2026 — providing up to $10,000 in matching grant funds to cover the cost of impact windows, impact doors, hurricane shutters, and roof reinforcement upgrades.

With $352 million in 2026 program funding now allocated by the Florida Legislature, the program is fully active — but history shows this money moves fast. Thousands of Central Florida homeowners have already benefited, and demand continues to outpace supply. If you own a home in the Orlando metropolitan area, understanding how to navigate this grant could save you tens of thousands of dollars in both upgrade costs and ongoing insurance premiums.

Why Central Florida Homeowners Need Impact Windows and Hurricane Protection Now

Many Orlando-area homeowners mistakenly believe that living inland eliminates hurricane risk. The reality is starkly different. Hurricanes Irma (2017) and Ian (2022) both caused catastrophic wind and structural damage deep into the Orlando metro area, hundreds of miles from the coast.

Beyond the physical threat, Central Florida is at the epicenter of Florida's insurance crisis. OIR-B1-1802: The New Wind Mitigation Form Changing Orlando Insurance Rates

Florida's Office of Insurance Regulation now requires insurers to use the updated OIR-B1-1802 wind mitigation inspection form, which places even greater weight on verified opening protections — specifically impact-rated windows and doors. This means a certified wind mitigation inspection following your MSFH-funded upgrade can now yield larger premium discounts than ever before for Orange, Osceola, Seminole, Lake, and Polk County homeowners. The state-required free inspection that comes with the MSFH program produces exactly this documentation, making it a dual-purpose financial tool.

What the My Safe Florida Home Grant Covers

The MSFH grant provides matching funds up to $10,000, meaning the state will match every dollar you spend on eligible improvements, dollar for dollar, up to the program cap. Covered improvements in the 2026 program cycle include:

  • Impact Windows — Florida Product Approved impact-resistant windows that meet or exceed Miami-Dade or Florida Building Code wind load requirements
  • Impact Doors — Front, back, sliding glass, and garage doors rated for high-velocity hurricane zones
  • Hurricane Shutters — Accordion shutters, roll-down shutters, panel shutters, and other code-compliant storm protection systems
  • Roof Deck Attachment and Roof-to-Wall Connection Reinforcement — Including secondary water barriers, enhanced roof deck fastening, and hurricane strap/clip upgrades for pre-code roof systems

Improvements must be installed by a state-licensed contractor and must follow the specific recommendations generated during your MSFH free home inspection. Florida Impact Shield holds all required state licenses and product approvals to ensure your installation qualifies for full reimbursement.

How to Apply for the My Safe Florida Home Grant: Step-by-Step for Orlando Homeowners

The MSFH application process involves several distinct phases. Here is exactly what Orlando-area homeowners need to do to claim their grant in 2026:

  1. Step 1 — Confirm Your Eligibility

    Review the eligibility checklist above. Confirm your homestead exemption status through your county property appraiser's website (orangecountyfl.net, property.osceola.org, scpafl.org, lakecoproperty.com, or polkpa.org depending on your county). Verify your home's assessed value falls below $700,000 and that your construction date precedes 2008.

  2. Step 2 — Create Your MSFH Portal Account

    Visit the official My Safe Florida Home website at mysafeflhome.com and create a homeowner account. You will need your property address, county parcel number, insurance policy number, and income documentation. Have these documents ready in digital format before you begin.

  3. Step 3 — Request Your Free Home Inspection

    Once your application is accepted, you will be assigned a state-approved inspector who will conduct a free wind mitigation inspection of your home. This inspection identifies which improvements are eligible under the program and generates the official report that drives your grant award. This inspection also produces your OIR-B1-1802 wind mitigation certificate, which you can immediately submit to your insurance carrier for a premium discount.

  4. Step 4 — Receive Your Grant Award Letter

    After the inspection, you will receive a grant award letter specifying the maximum grant amount you qualify for and the approved scope of improvements. Do not hire a contractor or begin any work until you have this letter in hand.

  5. Step 5 — Select a Licensed Contractor and Begin Improvements

    Choose a state-licensed contractor who is registered with the MSFH program. Florida Impact Shield is fully registered and licensed to complete MSFH-qualifying installations across all Central Florida counties. Work must be completed within the program's specified timeframe after your award letter is issued.

  6. Step 6 — Submit Completion Documentation and Receive Reimbursement

    After installation is complete, your contractor will submit completion certificates and permit documentation through the MSFH portal. Grant reimbursement is then processed directly by the state. The entire process from application approval to reimbursement typically takes 60–120 days depending on current program volume.

My Safe Florida Home Grant Orlando — Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get the My Safe Florida Home grant if I live in Kissimmee or Osceola County?

Yes. The My Safe Florida Home grant program is available to all eligible Florida homeowners regardless of county, and Osceola County residents in Kissimmee, Celebration, Poinciana, and St. Cloud are fully eligible. You must have an active homestead exemption through the Osceola County Property Appraiser, a home assessed at under $700,000, and a construction date prior to January 1, 2008. Florida Impact Shield services all Osceola County communities and can help you coordinate your application and installation.

How long does the My Safe Florida Home grant process take for Orlando homeowners?

From the time your application is submitted to the completion of your grant reimbursement, the process typically takes between 90 and 150 days for Orlando-area homeowners. The largest variable is inspector availability in your county. During peak application periods — such as immediately after a new funding round opens — inspection scheduling in Orange, Seminole, and Osceola counties can add four to six weeks to the timeline. Submitting your application as early as possible in the 2026 funding cycle is the most effective way to minimize delays.

Does the My Safe Florida Home grant cover impact windows for the whole house?

The grant covers impact window installations on openings identified in your state-approved wind mitigation inspection report. In most cases, this includes all exterior windows and glass doors. The grant amount is capped at $10,000 in matching funds, so the total coverage depends on the size of your home and the scope of work recommended by your inspector. For larger homes, many homeowners use the grant to fund the most critical openings first and finance the remainder. Visit our Orlando impact windows page for detailed information on product options and pricing.

What if my home was built after 2008 — can I still get help with hurricane protection?

Homes built after January 1, 2008 are generally not eligible for the My Safe Florida Home grant because they were constructed under the enhanced Florida Building Code that already incorporated many wind resistance requirements. However, homeowners with post-2008 construction can still benefit significantly from voluntary impact window and door upgrades, and may qualify for substantial insurance premium discounts through a wind mitigation inspection and OIR-B1-1802 certification. Florida Impact Shield provides impact window and door installations for all Central Florida homes regardless of construction date.

Is the My Safe Florida Home grant taxable income?

The My Safe Florida Home grant is generally not treated as taxable income for Florida homeowners because it is a reimbursement for qualified home improvement expenditures rather than earned compensation. However, tax treatment can vary based on individual circumstances, and the program has evolved over multiple legislative cycles. Florida Impact Shield strongly recommends consulting a licensed tax professional or CPA regarding your specific situation. The Florida Department of Financial Services and the program's official website also provide guidance on this question and any updates to program terms.
