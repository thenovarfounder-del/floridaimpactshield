Pompano Beach Hurricane Risk and Weather History

Pompano Beach's coastal location along the Atlantic Ocean places residents directly in the path of tropical systems that regularly threaten South Florida. The city has experienced numerous hurricane impacts throughout its history, with particularly devastating storms including Hurricane King in 1950, Hurricane Cleo in 1964, Hurricane Irma in 2017, and multiple near-misses that brought damaging winds and storm surge to the area.

The National Hurricane Center consistently identifies Broward County as one of Florida's highest-risk regions for hurricane landfall. Pompano Beach's proximity to warm Atlantic waters creates ideal conditions for rapid storm intensification, giving residents limited time to prepare when hurricanes approach. The 2024 hurricane season reinforced these concerns, with multiple named storms tracking dangerously close to the coastline.

Historical data reveals that Pompano Beach faces a hurricane threat approximately every 3-4 years on average, with major hurricanes (Category 3 or higher) impacting the area roughly once per decade. The city's low elevation and extensive waterfront properties make it particularly vulnerable to storm surge flooding, which can push water levels 10-15 feet above normal during major hurricane events.

High Velocity Hurricane Zone Requirements

Pompano Beach falls entirely within Florida's designated High Velocity Hurricane Zone, subjecting all window replacements and new construction to the state's most stringent building codes. These HVHZ requirements mandate that all windows withstand wind speeds exceeding 170 mph and resist impact from wind-borne debris traveling at high velocities.

Impact windows installed in Pompano Beach must pass rigorous testing protocols, including the large missile impact test where a 9-pound 2x4 lumber projectile strikes the window at 50 feet per second. Following impact, windows must maintain their structural integrity through 9,000 cycles of positive and negative pressure simulation, replicating the intense wind forces experienced during a major hurricane.

Choosing the Right Impact Windows for Your Pompano Beach Home
