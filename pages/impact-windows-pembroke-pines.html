Pembroke Pines falls entirely within Broward County's High Velocity Hurricane Zone, meaning all window installations must meet enhanced building codes designed for areas with extreme wind exposure. Our impact windows exceed these requirements, featuring:

  • Florida Product Approval numbers for all systems
  • NOA (Notice of Acceptance) documentation from Miami-Dade County

Frequently Asked Questions

▶ How long does impact window installation take in Pembroke Pines?

Most single-family homes in Pembroke Pines neighborhoods like Chapel Trail or Silver Lakes can be completed in 2-4 days, depending on the number of windows being replaced. We work efficiently while maintaining the highest quality standards, and we always clean up thoroughly after each day's work.

▶ Do impact windows really provide security benefits?

Absolutely. The same laminated glass technology that protects against hurricane debris also makes forced entry extremely difficult. Impact windows resist repeated blows that would shatter traditional glass, providing an effective deterrent against break-ins throughout Pembroke Pines neighborhoods.

▶ Will impact windows qualify me for My Safe Florida Home grants?

Yes, impact window installation is an eligible improvement under the MSFH program. Pembroke Pines homeowners can receive grants to help cover installation costs. We provide all documentation needed for your application and work with approved inspectors to ensure compliance.

▶ What makes HVHZ-rated impact windows different?

High Velocity Hurricane Zone windows undergo more rigorous testing for higher wind speeds and larger projectile impacts. Since all of Pembroke Pines is within the HVHZ, these enhanced specifications are required by code and ensure your windows can withstand the most severe storms our region faces.

▶ How soon will I see insurance savings after installation?

Once we provide your completion certificate and wind mitigation inspection report, you can submit these to your insurance company immediately. Most Pembroke Pines homeowners see premium reductions reflected at their next policy renewal, with savings continuing for the life of the windows.
