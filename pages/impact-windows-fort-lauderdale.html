Understanding HVHZ Requirements in Fort Lauderdale and Broward County

Fort Lauderdale and all coastal Broward County communities fall within the High Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ), which mandates the most stringent building standards in Florida. This designation requires impact windows to undergo rigorous testing protocols that simulate Category 5 hurricane conditions with wind speeds exceeding 155 mph and the impact of wind-borne debris traveling at dangerous velocities.

HVHZ Compliance Matters

Properties in Fort Lauderdale, Pompano Beach, Deerfield Beach, Hollywood, and Hallandale must meet HVHZ standards established by Miami-Dade and Broward County building departments. Only properly certified impact windows provide the protection and insurance benefits your property deserves.

The HVHZ encompasses areas within one mile of the coastline where wind speeds reach their highest intensity. Our impact windows for Broward County are specifically engineered to meet these elevated standards, featuring reinforced frames, laminated glass technology, and structural anchoring systems that create an impenetrable barrier against hurricane-force winds and flying debris.

Are impact windows required in Fort Lauderdale?

While not universally mandated for existing structures, impact windows or approved storm protection systems are required for all new construction and substantial renovations in Fort Lauderdale's HVHZ areas. Additionally, many insurance companies now require hurricane protection for policy coverage or offer significant discounts that make impact windows financially advantageous.

How much do impact windows cost in Fort Lauderdale?

Impact window costs vary based on size, style, glass specifications, and installation complexity. Fort Lauderdale homeowners typically invest between $650-$1,200 per window including professional installation. What's the difference between impact windows and hurricane shutters?

Impact windows provide permanent, maintenance-free protection that requires no storm preparation, while hurricane shutters must be deployed before each storm. Impact windows also deliver year-round benefits including energy efficiency, noise reduction, UV protection, and security that shutters cannot provide. Additionally, impact windows enhance property aesthetics and value far beyond what protective shutters offer.

How long does impact window installation take?

Most Fort Lauderdale residential impact window projects are completed within 3-7 days depending on the number of windows and any structural modifications required. Each window typically takes 2-4 hours to install properly. We schedule installations to minimize disruption and ensure your home remains secure throughout the process.

Do impact windows really reduce insurance premiums?

Yes, HVHZ-compliant impact windows qualify Fort Lauderdale homeowners for substantial insurance discounts, typically ranging from 30-45% on wind coverage portions of policies.
