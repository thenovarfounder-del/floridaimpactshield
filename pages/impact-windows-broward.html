Understanding Broward County's HVHZ Requirements

The High Velocity Hurricane Zone designation means that impact windows in Broward County must meet stringent testing standards outlined in the Florida Building Code. According to Florida Statute 553.77, all exterior opening protection in HVHZ areas must withstand wind speeds exceeding 170 mph and resist the impact of wind-borne debris traveling at extreme velocities.

Properties in Broward County must comply with ASTM E1996 and ASTM E1886 testing protocols, which simulate Category 5 hurricane conditions. This means your impact windows must pass both large missile impact tests (simulating a 9-pound 2x4 traveling at 50 feet per second) and small missile tests (simulating gravel and debris).

Important: Broward County requires a Notice of Acceptance (NOA) from the Florida Building Commission for all impact-resistant products. Our impact windows carry full NOA certification and meet all HVHZ requirements for Miami-Dade and Broward Counties.

My Safe Florida Home Grant Program

Broward County residents can access up to $10,000 in grant funding through the My Safe Florida Home program to offset the cost of impact window installation. This state-funded initiative, authorized under Florida Statute 215.5586, provides matching grants to homeowners who complete approved wind mitigation improvements.

Grant Eligibility Requirements

  • Your home must be your primary residence
  • The property must have a insured value of $300,000 or less (or meet income requirements for higher values)
  • Your home must have been built before 2009
  • You must complete a free home inspection through the program
  • All work must be performed by licensed contractors like Florida Impact Shield
