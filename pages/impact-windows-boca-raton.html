Boca Raton's Hurricane Exposure and Storm History

Located in southern Palm Beach County along Florida's Atlantic coast, Boca Raton sits directly in one of the nation's most hurricane-prone regions. The city's prime oceanfront location places it at heightened risk for devastating hurricane impacts, with wind speeds potentially exceeding 150 mph during major storms. Historical data reveals that Boca Raton has experienced numerous significant hurricane events, including Hurricane Wilma in 2005, which caused extensive property damage throughout Palm Beach County with sustained winds of 120 mph and catastrophic storm surge.

The Atlantic hurricane season, running from June through November, poses an annual threat to Boca Raton properties. Climate scientists project increasing hurricane intensity in coming decades, making comprehensive structural protection more critical than ever. Impact windows provide the first line of defense against hurricane-force winds, flying debris, and the catastrophic pressure changes that can literally tear roofs from homes when windows fail during storms.

Palm Beach County's building codes reflect this serious hurricane exposure, requiring impact-resistant protection for all new construction and many renovation projects. Homeowners throughout Boca Raton increasingly recognize that impact windows aren't merely building code compliance—they're essential investments in property protection, family safety, and long-term financial security. Substantial Insurance Savings for Boca Raton Homeowners

My Safe Florida Home Grants Available to Boca Raton Residents
